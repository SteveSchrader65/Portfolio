Overview

Hello and welcome to my online portfolio, where innovation meets know-how in the world of programming and web-development. I am excited to present a showcase of my skills and projects, developed over the past few years after making the life-altering decision to return to full-time studies.

Hi !! I'm Steve ...
Having worked numerous roles in the transport industry over a twenty-year period, I found that I had reached a cross-road in my life and looked to pursue an interest in computer programming which had been germinating - unfulfilled - since my teenage years.

The COVID pandemic had not yet arrived when the decision was made to take-up studies again. So it could be said that your presenter today was one of the innovators at the forefront of the current generational change towards working remotely.
Personally, I think my role was mere circumstance.

This web-site was originally intended to serve merely as a launching platform for projects and assessments worked-on during my studies. However, I soon realized that I also needed some form of portfolio through which I could showcase my work and projects to potential employers and clients. And so I looked to re-design my site. I re-built it from the ground up, planning to let it grow and evolve as I learned new skills.

Throughout my journey, I have worked to hone my proficiency in various programming languages, frameworks and development tools. From crafting dynamic and responsive websites to developing robust database solutions and mobile applications, I trust that my portfolio reflects my adaptability and commitment to working on a diverse range of projects with your organization.

Studies

Prior to this recent journey, I had twice attempted similar programming courses on a part-time basis. However for me - with a full-time job and attending evening classes, as well as trying to find the time to study and complete assessments - there simply were not enough hours in any given week. I needed to be able to dedicate myself 100% !!
Then ... early in 2019 an opportunity arose ... and so I took it.
(Click/Select any qualification award for additional information)

Beginning with the CertIV in Programming in June, 2019, we learned of basic programming structures using Java, and were introduced to Object-Oriented Programming concepts and principles. Database programming with SQL Server was the core of one unit, introducing CRUD operations. During another unit I created my first Unity game. Due to restrictions caused by the pandemic, it took until the end of 2020 to complete this course.

Next I looked to the CertIV in Web Development to address my complete lack of knowledge in HTML, CSS and JavaScript. In this course I was to learn the Python programming language; it also exposed me to SEO and its' relationship with e-commerce, as well as providing a solid basis in cyber-security and ethical business practices. My second Certificate-level qualification was completed in the middle of 2022 and I next had to decide if I wished to pursue a Programming stream, or further explore the Web Development branch.

Believing that programming had the potential to open up more career opportunities, in 2022 I began the Diploma of Advanced Programming. This new diploma course was heavily programming-intensive, covering more studies with PHP and database programming in Laravel and MongoDB. There were further units in Python, as well as IoT programming (even more Python!!). Data Analysis formed a core part of the second semester where we were introduced to PowerBI, and Python packages, such as numpy and pandas. However, we were to spend the majority of this second semester with Dart and Flutter, working on our final group project - a mobile application.

Since ending formal studies, I have aimed to further my knowledge with online tutorials and courses in HTML/CSS and JavaScript frameworks such as React and TypeScript. Indeed, this website was created from scratch using learned skills and (where necessary) researching features that I wished to incorporate. (My current plan is to re-do this site in React, once I gain more proficiency with that JavaScript framework.) At time of writing, it is my intention to enrol in the Diploma of Web Development in 2024.

  • Completed at the end of 2020, Clive ... of the Dead was my first assessment using the Unity package. Clive was a 2D scrolling platformer. Coding for the game was written in C#. In the game, our hero must deal with swooping bats that will awaken when he moves beneath them, and then continuously attack until Clive can run/jump far enough away to evade their sonar.

    In addition to the bats, Clive had to contend with spiders and rats whilst trying to collect as much treasure as he can before making good his escape. The game has a few 'Easter Eggs' in the form of humourous head-stones which could be discovered for extra points.

  • The Recipe Book app was assigned early in 2021. This database application was written entirely in C#, with local database storage. Recipe Book allows users to store and retrieve recipes, as well as creating and editting new ones. Personal favourites could be marked with a 'Faves' star, which could be accessed through a menu system.

    This project took up a considerable amount of my study-time early in 2021. I had a vision of how I wanted the app to look and perform; trying to match this goal led to numerous late night/early morning coding sessions, and even had me reaching-out to SyncFusion(USA) for assistance.

  • The Life & Boats of CaptainSteve was my first web-site to be hosted live. We learned about Search Engine Optimization (SEO) and how to connect with Google to interface with actual web-users. Fuelled by my recently discovered interest in JavaScript, I created a somewhat unique landing page for this site.

    In addition to the JavaScript code, CaptainSteve was written using HTML, CSS and PHP. You can drop by and view my early work via the Links tab at the top of this portfolio. The site was first launched in 2022.

  • Anagrams was an assessment from my 2023 unit on Data Analytics and Power BI. This assignment continued my introduction to Python packages such as numpy and pandas. My submission began by indexing a dictionary file containing more than 200,000 entries. The user would be prompted to enter a word and the application would search to see if the word was present. Additionally, the programme would also search a pre-indexed dataframe to list any and all anagrams of the user's word.

  • My Video Player app was built as an assessment for the Advanced Python programming class in 2023. The remit was to construct an app that could search a local file system for image and video files, and then display and play these. The project was written in Python using the PyQt5 library.

  • During the first semester we had been formed into teams and allocated projects which were to be a large part of our final assessments. Initially, my team was assigned the PLUMS project and I spent a considerable portion of my 2021/ 2022 break working on the Laravel database section. However, I was later re-assigned to another project as our class size had suffered considerably due to attrition.
